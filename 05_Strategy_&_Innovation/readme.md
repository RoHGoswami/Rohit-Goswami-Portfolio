# Strategy and Innovation Project: "Terre Milanesi" Two-Sided Digital Platform
## Project Context
- Course: Leadership & Innovation, A.Y. 2022-2023 (Politecnico di Milano).
- Client: Comune di Milano.
- Focus: Food Policy, Sustainability, Digital Platform Strategy, Two-Sided Markets.
- Objective: Develop a process innovation tool to increase the consumption of fresh, local, and sustainable agricultural products among Milan's residents.

The Business Challenge Despite Milan having one of the largest agricultural parks in Europe, local farmers' markets accounted for only 2% of families' consumption, while supermarkets dominated at 74%. The team identified several core barriers:
- Consumer Friction: Citizens lacked time, easy access, and information regarding market locations, operating hours, and product seasonality.
- Farmer Constraints: Farmers struggled to compete with large distributors, lacked direct communication channels with end consumers, and often had to sell seasonal overstock at halved prices.

## The Solution: To bridge this gap, the team designed "Terre Milanesi," a non-profit, two-sided digital platform connecting local farmers directly with consumers.
-	Digital Accessibility: Features an interactive map of markets, detailed farmer profiles, and product catalogs with transparent pricing.
-	"MyBox" Subscription Model: A fidelity program allowing users to subscribe to periodic "mystery boxes" of seasonal products directly from their preferred farmers.

### Analysis & Implementation Plan
1.	Market & Customer Research:
- Conducted PESTEL and SWOT analyses to map the macro-environment.
- Surveyed consumers to uncover latent needs, revealing that 87.3% liked the experience of local markets, but required better information and convenience.
- Developed Customer Personas (e.g., the busy working mother, the local farmer) and mapped out their User Journeys to prioritize UX/UI features.

2. Go-to-Market Strategy:
- Addressed the "chicken and egg" problem of two-sided platforms by applying a "Marquee strategy"—onboarding farmers first through direct Municipality outreach, then launching consumer marketing.
- Applied a subscription pricing strategy leveraging the "decoy effect" to encourage higher-volume purchases, ensuring steady revenue streams for producers.

3.	Cost Structure:
- Projected a €403,080 budget for the first year, including app development (UX/UI, developers, QA), maintenance, and a robust €182,500 social media advertising plan.

### Key Outcomes
- Strategic Alignment: The solution directly supported the Milan Food Policy's goals of ensuring healthy food access, promoting sustainability, and improving food education.
- Financial Impact for Farmers: Modeled a "Best Case Scenario" showing that shifting distribution toward direct app subscriptions and markets could increase farmers' unitary profit margins from €1.395/u to €1.666/u.
- Team Leadership: Successfully navigated the "Tuckman Model" (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning) to manage a diverse, cross-cultural team of 6 students. Utilized the "Whole Brain Model" to balance the team's logical, organized, interpersonal, and holistic skill sets under a shared leadership framework.
